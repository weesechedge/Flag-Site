A Flag for the Republic

A flag that speaks to who we are, not who we were.

Background

As discussions about becoming a fully independent republic gain momentum, we have an opportunity to adopt a flag that reflects who we are today — confident, inclusive, and forward‑looking. Our current flag, while historically significant, tells a story - or rather, makes a statement - that was never really about us, but about Britain. This new design offers a symbol that resonates with the entire nation, reflecting who we are today while honouring the history that truly was ours.

Why This Flag?

  • With Golden Soil: The golden field represents the rich land we are fortunate to have, and that we must guard and cultivate for our future prosperity.
  • A Sunburnt Country: The dry green field is the signature colour of our environment, representative of this vast land, its age, its wisdom, and its history.
  • Eureka: The Southern Cross, evolved to use Eureka stars, represents our evolution while honouring an essential symbol of Australia. The six points of the star representing our initial six States, with the seventh and eighth points representing our internal and external territories. The Eureka star is truly our symbol, a decisive statement of our individuality and self-determination.
  • The Golden Wattle: The flag incorporates our national floral emblem, as a ring of petals surrounding the Eureka star. This unique symbol also embeds the notion of a coming-together, echoing indigenous symbols for meeting places, which represents our coming together as many peoples to form a cohesive and inclusive nation.
  • A Land of Promise: The stars on the golden field also serve as a visual metaphor for this country's history of migration and multiculturalism. The stars are those who journeyed to the land, represented as the green field, before the coming together (the Wattle) to reconcile and share in this nation.

Why Now?

The move toward a republic is more than a constitutional amendment, it’s a declaration that our destiny is our own. A new flag is a chance to tell the world, and remind ourselves, that the next chapter of the Australian story is being written by all of us, together.

Self‑Determination

Embrace full sovereignty with colours and symbols that are undeniably ours.

Modernisation

The flag of modern Australia should symbolise who we are - through celebrating Australian colours, land, flora, symbols, and our history.

Global Identity

Stand confidently on the world stage with an emblem unmistakably Australian.

A Vision for the Future

Imagine the first day the new flag unfurls above Parliament House: children discovering its meaning in classrooms, athletes carrying it with pride, and new citizens pledging allegiance beneath its colours. It is more than cloth - it is our shared promise.
